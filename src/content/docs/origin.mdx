The acronym arrived first.

**APE** — *Agent Persistence Exocortex.* It read like a name a serious system would carry. The expansion came after, and to the system's credit, every word still belongs.

*Agent*, because the thing is built to serve agents — not chat sessions, not retrieval pipelines, not document corpora. *Persistence*, because the entire premise is that memory should outlive the process that wrote it. *Exocortex*, because what APE is, structurally, is a brain you can detach: graph and corpus on disk, queryable from anywhere, surviving every model migration in front of it.

The portrait above is, of course, an editorial liberty. APE does not in fact wear a smoking jacket, take notes by firelight, or drink from a branded mug. It runs as a Rust binary alongside SQLite and Qdrant, and its taste in jackets is unrecorded.

But the image carries something true. The point of an exocortex is composure — a record kept carefully enough that the agent reading it can act with the bearing of someone who has been here before. Memory, dressed for the occasion.
